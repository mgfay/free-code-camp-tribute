  • 1876 - Born in Kurume, Fukuoka, in Kyushu
  • 1895 - Sent to Kyoto to study under Tamura Shoryu
  • 1898 - Moves to Tokyo to study under Koyama Shōtarō
  • 1899 - Has first American exhibition at the Detroit Museum of Art
  • 1907 - Marries Fujio Yoshida (吉田 ふじを)
  • 1907 - Has first son, Tōshi Yoshida (吉田 遠志)
  • 1920 - Presents first woodcut at the Watanabe Print Workshop
  • 1925 Second son, Hodaka Yoshida (吉田 穂高), is born
  • 1926 Establishes own studio
  • 1939 Publishes "Japanese Woodblock Printing", a guide to the art of woodblock printing
  • 1946 Designs last print
  • 1950 Paints last two pieces: "The Sea of Western Izu" and "The Mountains of Izu"
  • 1950 Dies at the age of 73
